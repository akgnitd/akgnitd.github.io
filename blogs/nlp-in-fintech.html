NLP in FinTech

In the lending industry, risk assessment traditionally relies on manual document review — a process that's slow, error-prone, and difficult to scale. We set out to change that by applying Natural Language Processing (NLP) to automate the extraction and analysis of critical information from financial documents.

The Challenge

Financial documents — bank statements, salary slips, ITR forms — come in wildly different formats. Traditional rule-based extraction breaks down when faced with this variability. We needed a system that could understand the structure and semantics of these documents, not just pattern-match on fixed templates.

Our Approach

We built custom Named Entity Recognition (NER) models using Python and spaCy, trained on a corpus of financial documents specific to the Indian lending market. The model identifies entities like employer names, salary amounts, account numbers, and transaction patterns with high accuracy.

The pipeline combines OCR (for scanned documents), NER (for entity extraction), and a classification layer (for document type detection) into a single end-to-end service. Everything runs as a microservice with configurable feature flags, allowing us to enable or disable individual processing stages based on the use case.

Impact

The results were significant: 3x increase in document processing throughput while maintaining 98% accuracy in entity extraction. What previously took a human reviewer 15 minutes per application now completes in under 10 seconds. The system processes thousands of applications daily, directly impacting approval speed and customer experience.

The key takeaway: domain-specific NLP models, when trained on the right data, vastly outperform generic solutions. The investment in building a custom training pipeline paid for itself within the first quarter of deployment.
