The EM Shift

Transitioning from a Staff Engineer to an Engineering Manager (EM) is one of the most significant shifts in a software career. It’s not a promotion to "Senior Engineer Plus"—it’s a completely different role that requires a new set of skills and, more importantly, a new mindset.

From Fixing Code to Empowering People

As a technical lead, your impact is measured by the quality of your architectural designs and the complexity of the bugs you resolve. As an EM, your impact is multiplied by your team’s success. The hardest part of the shift is learning to step back and resist the urge to jump into every PR. Instead, you must focus on empowering your engineers to find the right solutions themselves.

Managing Technical Debt

In a fast-growing FinTech startup like Credit Saison, the pressure to deliver features is immense. The "EM Shift" means becoming the guardian of long-term sustainability. You have to balance the product roadmap with the health of the codebase, making the hard calls on when to refactor and when to build fast.

The Psychology of Engineering Leadership

Leadership is about empathy as much as it is about technology. Understanding what motivates individual engineers—whether it's learning a new stack like Go or taking on more architectural ownership—is key to building a high-performance culture.

My journey to Engineering Manager has taught me that while code is predictable, people and processes are complex. Embracing that complexity is what makes the role so rewarding.
