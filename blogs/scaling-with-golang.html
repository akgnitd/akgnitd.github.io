Scaling with Golang

In the fast-paced world of FinTech, performance isn't just a metric — it's a requirement. At Credit Saison, we faced a significant challenge: our core validation engines were struggling to handle the increasing volume of real-time transactions with the low latency our users expect. After extensive benchmarking, we made the strategic decision to transition mission-critical services to Golang.

Why Golang?

Java has been our backbone for years, but the overhead of JVM and the complexity of managing thread pools at extreme scales became a bottleneck. Go's lightweight Goroutines allowed us to handle thousands of concurrent requests using minimal memory compared to traditional thread-based models.

The Performance Gains

The results were immediate. We observed a 40% reduction in P99 latency. More importantly, our infrastructure costs dropped as we could process the same number of requests with fewer CPU and RAM resources. The simplicity of Go's concurrency model also reduced the bugs associated with complex multi-threaded Java applications.

Lessons Learned

Transitioning a team isn't just about syntax; it's about mindset. Moving from an OOP-heavy Spring Boot environment to Go's composition-based approach required a cultural shift. We focused on clear documentation and peer reviews to ensure our "Go-idiomatic" code remained maintainable and scalable.

Today, Go powers our most critical validation layers, enabling us to deliver lightning-fast financial decisions for millions of users.
